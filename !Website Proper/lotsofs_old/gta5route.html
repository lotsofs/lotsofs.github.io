Definition

The general idea is: If at the end of the entire playthrough, there will be evidence that you have done a thing, that thing is required. Otherwise, it's not. OR if you can load any save and somehow figure out whether that save has done a thing or not, that thing is required. Otherwise, it's not. Making people watch a 24 hour video just to prove to them: “Look I did it” should not be needed.

Game Version

New Gen (PC, Xbone, PS4) with returning player sans DLC. Version 1.27

To adjust to your own version: Simply exclude everything that isn't present in your version.

Category Name

I think 'true 100%' is not a good category name, because what does 'true' even mean? ‘100%+’ seems like a much less ambiguous term that isn’t unheard of already either. There could maybe be a varying amount of +'s depending on the game version, which affects content: Old gen, New gen, New gen w/ returning players reward.

Leaderboards

As far as leaderboards go, it's too early to even consider making those right now. If in the distant future one does end up being made, I think the use of variables and flags should be a necessity to differentiate between different game versions and possibly different definitions of 'the category that involves doing more than 100%'.

These should definitely be required, based on what previous GTAs were like, or because they’re some of the most obviously trackable things.

  • Everything on the social club checklist page.*
  • 69 Missions
  • 61 Hobbies and Pastimes
  • 58 Strangers and Freaks (referred to as ‘Quests’ in this doc. (Short for ‘Question Mark Missions’))

  • 30 Misc

  • All properties purchased
  • All epsilon tracts
  • 27 peyote plants

*This page includes missable stuff, like Simeon's random event, and all of Michael/Trevor's S&F missions.

2 - The Reasonable

I think these should be required, but if you have a good reason I might be convinced for them not to. "It's dumb" is not a good reason and I don't fully agree with it. Paramedic in GTA3D era is dumb too, and that’s not even a 100% + thing.

  • Getting gold medals on all missions/races/shooting range/etc
  • Clearing the altruist camp
  • Michael's three family missions, triggered by phonecall
  • The last therapy session, where you can kill Dr. Friendlander
  • Talking to all the special characters which appear with a white blip on the map (these can be used in director mode after)
  • A 28th bonus peyote plant (does this exist?)
  • The Murder Mystery thing for returning players
  • Completing all the challenges for the hunting minigame.
  • The silly children of the mountain website minigame which gives a t-shirt
  • Completing the Rail Gun ammunation challenge

3 - The Dumb

I really don't want these to be required, because they're dumb (some more than others), but that's not a good reason, so they should be required. Please convince me otherwise:

  • Unlocking all the golf opponents. Involves playing golf 3 times. Beating them too, if this leaves any checkmarks or the likes.
  • Getting a checkmark next to all weapons in the shooting range. Involves starting the challenge with all of these then backing out immediately.
  • Max Stats. In GTAO these decrease over time, however they don't seem to in SP. Someone confirm/refute this, it would be a good reason to exclude this.
  • Purchase/collect all clothing
  • Unlock and keep alive all heist members
  • Keep all three protagonists alive, in other words Ending C. This one doesn’t matter, since you’d have to do all three missions using the replay menu anyway. Not to mention that Trevor has post-finale S&F missions so his ending is out of the question anyway.
  • Collect all the special outfits. Most notably: Triathlon outfits require completion of a triathlon with each character.
  • All of the category 1 and 2 stuff that are tracked and rewarded per character and therefore can be done three times (eg. Shooting Range & Flight School)

6 - The Speedrun Rules

These are self imposed restrictions commonly applied in many speedruns of this game. Apply to your own liking.

  • Taxis: I don’t mind them much for long distance skipping, but they really dumb down routing for collectibles and short distance drives. I might go for not using them. Will decide later. Using these on missions often voids a particular Gold Medal requirement, so often these aren’t viable anyway, but they’re still useful for in-between missions. This stat is tracked by ‘Distance traveled as a taxi passenger’ in the stats and on Social Club.
  • Missions Skips: These are particularly dumb, but often they void many Gold Medal requirements, so their use would be limited. Still, it might be more fun to ‘ban’ these altogether for the few cases where they do come in useful. (That also saves me having to test shit).
  • OM0 Mission Skip Instapassing: I haven’t tested anything with this, but I imagine these too void many Gold Medal rules. They might be just plain not worth going for at all. Though more elaborate than missions skips, I might consider using these.
  • Director Mode Shenanigans: I think this breaks the entire game? And in bad ways too. I’m not sure the main speedrunning community even settled on this yet. Safer to not use these.
  • Other warps: Hangout warps/save warps/etc.: These are much more limited in possibilities so I will be using them.

7 - The Forgotten

I may have forgotten stuff, this game does have a fair share of content, most of which is hidden. Let me know if I did.

My approach

To come up with this definition, I took a list on GTA Wikia (things not required for 100%), added stuff to it that I know existed but wasn't on there, then proceeded to do those things in a casual playthrough to see if they were tracked somehow. If after I did them there was evidence that I've done them, I would mark them as required. If there was no evidence, I would mark them as not required. This seemed to me to be the most solid definition with the least amount of gray area, however it does also come with a lot of silly shit, like buying all the clothes. At first i considered 'what would R* have decided if this was GTA3?', but it left too much grey area.
